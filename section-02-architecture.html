MLOps Operating Model

This is how I would structure the platform so product teams can ship with less operational drag. The emphasis is reliability first, product usefulness second, and infrastructure complexity only where it earns its keep.

How I Would Help
Make the platform easier to trust, easier to ship, and easier to extend.

My value is in turning data and ML workflows into dependable product infrastructure: quality gates, repeatable deployment, clear lineage, and pragmatic support for AI where it actually helps the product.

Use-case-first execution with systems that scale cleanly
That means starting with small, useful capabilities, building calm operating habits around them, and only productizing what proves its value.

① Data source
Higg FEM / FSLM Assessments
Core business input and the foundation for downstream reliability
What it is

The Higg Facility Environmental Module (FEM) collects quantitative data on energy, water, waste, chemicals, and GHG from factories. The FSLM covers social and labor. These are direct operating inputs, which is exactly why the platform needs strong data handling before downstream reporting or AI can be trusted.

The infrastructure problem

Primary assessment data, operational updates, and supporting evidence create the kind of input complexity that can quietly break reporting and AI. This is why I would start with a governed ingestion path and a reproducible storage model before trying to expand downstream intelligence.

Business value
Core product inputRegulatory workflowsPlatform trust
① Acquisition data
GoBlu Chemical & Environmental Data
Worldly's acquired chemical management platform ZDHC records, environmental signals, facility chemical inventories
What GoBlu adds to the data model

GoBlu's acquisition brings structured chemical inventory data ZDHC Gateway conformance records, hazardous substance declarations, and facility-level environmental discharge signals into the Worldly data model. This is a different schema, a different ingestion pattern, and a different compliance context from Higg FEM. Merging it into the lakehouse without breaking existing dbt lineage requires schema evolution support. Iceberg's in-place column additions handle this without a table rewrite.

The infrastructure challenge

Acquisition data arrives with its own historical schema, ID conventions, and update cadence. The ingestion layer needs to map GoBlu facility IDs to Worldly's canonical facility records without data loss and Great Expectations needs new contract definitions for chemical record completeness before any GoBlu data reaches a CSRD report or product score. This is a data quality problem before it's an engineering problem.

Business value
Chemical compliance signalsCSRD scope expansionZDHC gateway coverage
① Data source
Supplier APIs & Operational Feeds
Multi-tier supply chain data from tier 1 through tier 4
What it includes

Beyond Higg assessments: production volume data, material provenance, chemical inventories, third-party audit results, and carbon intensity measurements. This kind of multi-tier data footprint is one reason I would keep the ingestion and governance model explicit rather than ad hoc.

The China constraint

Regional data handling rules can create situations where supplier or factory data should not leave a specific jurisdiction. I would treat that as a product and infrastructure constraint early, not as a deployment detail to clean up later.

① Data source
Audit Reports & Certificates
Unstructured documents the hardest data to process, the most valuable for compliance
What it includes

Third-party audit reports, chemical safety data sheets, Bluesign and ZDHC certifications, supplier code of conduct sign-offs. These are PDFs and scanned documents no structured schema, inconsistent formats, multi-language. They contain some of the most important compliance signals.

Why this is hard

You can't run a SQL query over a scanned audit PDF. These documents need extraction, classification, and linking to the structured facility records before they're useful. This is where NiFi + document parsing (Unstructured.io or similar) feeds the lakehouse with unstructured content.

Business value
CSRD evidenceAI search target
① Data source
LCA Datasets & Regulatory Reference
Life-cycle assessment data that powers the Product Impact Calculator
What it is

Life Cycle Assessment data quantifies environmental impact across a material's entire production chain from raw extraction through processing, dyeing, and transport. The Higg Materials Sustainability Index (MSI) contains LCA data for hundreds of materials. Combined with facility performance data, this powers product-level Scope 3 carbon calculations.

The data join challenge

LCA data (structured, numeric) must join with facility assessment data (semi-structured) and product BOM specs (hierarchical) to produce a product-level impact score. This multi-source join across different data models is exactly what dbt handles in the transformation layer.

Business value
Product Impact CalculatorPEFCR compliance
② Ingestion
Apache NiFi
Guaranteed-delivery document ingestion for compliance-sensitive evidence
Why I would consider it

For document-heavy evidence flows, I would prefer a tool that treats delivery guarantees, retries, and provenance as first-class concerns rather than afterthoughts. That matters more here than having the most fashionable ingestion stack.

Why NiFi for Worldly

NiFi is designed for guaranteed-delivery, document-oriented dataflow with explicit failure handling. Audit PDFs, certificates, and supplier documents need to arrive in the lakehouse reliably a dropped document that doesn't generate an error is worse than a visible failure. NiFi routes failures to dead-letter queues with full provenance. You know exactly what failed, why, and what the data was.

Business value
No silent data lossFull audit trailCompliance-grade ingestion

② Transform
dbt Data Build Tool
Version-controlled transformation layer for reviewable, auditable business logic
Why I would consider it

I would prefer a transformation layer that keeps logic versioned, reviewable, and visible to the broader team. The main value is a cleaner operating model for shared analytical logic.

Why it wins

Auditor-ready lineage is the real reason to use dbt here. Every CSRD figure becomes traceable to its raw source, every transformation is version-controlled, and every logic change is reviewable. When an auditor asks how a number was produced, the answer is a model lineage path, not institutional memory.

Business value
Auditor-ready lineageVersion-controlled logic
② Validation
Great Expectations
Data quality gates that keep bad inputs out of downstream systems
Why I would start here

I would rather put effort into explicit quality contracts than debug downstream surprises later. Validation rules, quarantines, and visible failures create a much better operating model than quietly accepting bad data and discovering the damage in reporting or ML outputs.

Why it wins

Automated integrity gates matter more than marginal model gains. If corrupted ESG inputs reach reporting or ML pipelines, Worldly risks bad scores, broken trust, and downstream regulatory exposure for customers. Great Expectations lets the platform quarantine bad batches before they contaminate the source of truth.

Business value
Prevents bad compliance outputsQuarantine vs silent fail
③ Storage
MinIO
S3-compatible object storage recommended for sovereignty and portability
Why I would bias this way

I would choose MinIO when portability and deployment symmetry matter more than maximizing convenience inside one cloud. The point is to preserve one storage pattern across regions and avoid turning the data layer into a long-term constraint.

Why it wins

Global parity is the real benefit. MinIO is S3-compatible, open-source, and self-hostable on any cloud, so the same application pattern can run in the US, EU, and China without forking the storage layer. That directly supports sovereign data handling while keeping the engineering model unified.

Business value
China data sovereigntySingle codebase, all regionsNo S3 lock-in
③ Table format
Apache Iceberg
Open table format with schema evolution and time travel recommended for long-lived analytical data
Why I would bias this way

I would choose Iceberg when the platform needs to absorb schema change without turning every update into a migration project. The value is not novelty. It is a calmer operating model for long-lived analytical data.

Why it wins

Future-proof schema and time travel are the reasons this belongs in Worldly's core. Higg methodologies change annually, and audit questions often arrive months later. Iceberg handles evolving schemas without painful migrations and lets Worldly reproduce exactly what a dataset looked like on a prior date when an auditor or customer needs proof.

Business value
Annual methodology updates without migrationsTime travel for audits
③ Query
Trino + ClickHouse
Federated query plane and sub-second analytics across the ESG lakehouse
Why two engines, not one

Trino and ClickHouse solve different problems. Trino federates it joins across Iceberg tables, PostgreSQL operational DB, and external sources without moving data, which eliminates the fragile export pipelines Worldly currently runs. ClickHouse aggregates it delivers sub-second GROUP BY queries over billions of rows for the Insights Hub. My PostgreSQL optimisation work (800ms → 200ms via EXPLAIN ANALYZE, covering indexes, N+1 elimination at RoundTechSquare) applies directly to the query planning principles both engines use.

Why it wins

The value here is federated insight without export sprawl. Trino joins facility performance, LCA, and product data across systems without creating duplicate pipelines just to answer one question. ClickHouse supplies the sub-second aggregation layer the Insights Hub needs for an executive-grade analytics experience.

Business value
Product Impact CalculatorReal-time Insights Hub
④ Orchestration
Dagster
Asset-based orchestration for better visibility into what changed and what needs to run
Why I would choose this pattern

I would prefer orchestration that is aware of data assets, not just scheduled tasks. That makes it easier for teams to see what changed, what is stale, and what needs to be recomputed when upstream data or logic moves.

Why Dagster over Airflow

Airflow schedules tasks. Dagster tracks assets. That distinction is critical for Worldly: when a raw Higg assessment batch updates, Dagster automatically surfaces which downstream dbt models, feature tables, and model scores are stale and need recomputation. With Airflow you'd wire that lineage manually and maintain it every time the schema changes. For a compliance platform where audit reproducibility is a product requirement, asset-aware orchestration is not a preference it's the right architecture.

Business value
Automatic retry on failureNative data lineageCSRD reproducibility built in
④ Experiments
MLflow
Model tracking and registry for reproducibility and controlled change
Why I would insist on it

If model behavior influences product or reporting workflows, I would want every promoted model to be traceable, reviewable, and easy to roll back. That is less about ML sophistication and more about dependable product operations.

Why this matters for regulatory outputs

When Worldly's Product Impact Calculator produces a Scope 3 estimate that appears in a CSRD report, that figure must be reproducible and explainable months later. MLflow's model registry logs the exact model version, training data hash, hyperparameters, and environment for every production model. If an auditor questions a figure 12 months later, the answer exists in MLflow.

Business value
Audit-ready model versionsReproducible experiments

Supplier chemical inventories, social audit data, and facility-level business performance cannot be sent to a third-party embedding API. Self-hosted models via HuggingFace keep all data within Worldly's infrastructure contractually clean and China-region compatible.

④ Inference
vLLM
Self-hosted high-concurrency LLM serving China-compatible, zero data egress
Why vLLM over SageMaker or TGI

I would choose an inference layer like vLLM when the team needs private serving, reasonable concurrency, and the option to run outside a single cloud vendor's managed path. The main value is operational flexibility, not simply raw model throughput.

Why it wins

vLLM is about private scale. It supports high-concurrency AI serving while keeping supplier and facility data inside Worldly-controlled infrastructure. That combination matters because the assistant must be both broadly usable during peak season and acceptable for sovereign or contract-sensitive data.

⑤ RAG
Vector DB Qdrant
Vector search for retrieval workflows that need scoped, relevant context
Why I would use it carefully

I would introduce vector search only when there is a clear retrieval use case that benefits from it. The value is not in having a vector database. The value is in grounding search and AI workflows with better context and clearer scope.

Why Qdrant specifically

Qdrant is open-source, self-hostable, and has strong multi-tenancy for isolating vector spaces by application (Worldly Assistant, product scoring, supplier risk) without running separate instances. It supports metadata filtering alongside vector search critical for scoping "find relevant Higg guidance for FEM section 3 questions" vs "find methodology text for chemical management." I'd evaluate Qdrant vs Milvus before committing either would work.

⑤ Monitoring
Evidently AI Drift Monitoring
Production ML monitoring for compliance-facing model outputs
Why Evidently AI specifically

I would favor monitoring that stays close to the platform, plugs into existing orchestration, and makes drift visible before it becomes a product issue. The point is operational awareness, not another standalone dashboard.

What gets monitored and why it's regulatory

The Product Impact Calculator's Scope 3 estimates appear in legally-mandated CSRD disclosures. I monitor input feature drift separately from prediction drift the distinction matters for diagnosis: stable features with drifting predictions signals a model failure; both drifting signals an upstream data distribution shift. Either fires an alert before an inaccurate figure reaches a regulatory report. That's legal liability prevention, not product quality.

Business value
Prevents inaccurate CSRD filingsIntegrated with Dagster checksZero data egress

A shared runtime model that keeps deployment behavior consistent across environments
Why I would keep the runtime consistent

I would rather give teams one deployment model they can learn well than several environment-specific ones they have to keep reinterpreting. Shared runtime behavior reduces operational surprise and makes delivery calmer.

Why it wins

Global parity is the key outcome. The same Kubernetes deployment model can be adapted for US, EU, and China environments with region-specific configuration rather than separate codebases. That keeps operational muscle memory unified while respecting data residency constraints.

⑥ IaC
Terraform
Infrastructure as code so environments are reviewable, reproducible, and less person-dependent
Why I would insist on it

I would want infrastructure changes to be reviewed like code, repeated safely across environments, and understandable by more than one person. That makes scaling the team easier and lowers operational risk.

Why it wins

Terraform makes global consistency auditable. The same baseline can be applied across providers, which reduces regional drift and makes security or compliance hardening repeatable. For a company operating across sovereign boundaries, that consistency is an executive control advantage, not just an engineering convenience.

Business value

Automated deployment path for safer, more routine change management
Why I would put this in early

I would make change safer before making it faster. Once tests, deploy checks, and rollback expectations are part of the normal path, the team can move with much more confidence.

Why it wins

The point of CI/CD here is controlled change. Every model or transformation update can be tested, benchmarked, and phased into production with rollback protection. That lowers the odds of a silent regression reaching a customer-facing report or scoring workflow.

⑥ Security
HashiCorp Vault + Apache DataHub
Secrets management, column-level access control, and data lineage catalog
Why governance is a first-class component

Security, governance, and versioning should be designed into the platform rather than layered on later. Vault centralizes secrets, while access controls keep sensitive operational data separate from broader analytical use.

DataHub as the data catalog

Apache DataHub provides a searchable lineage catalog across all Iceberg tables, dbt transformations, and ML model inputs. When an auditor asks "where did this CSRD figure come from?", the answer is a DataHub lineage graph not a tribal knowledge walkthrough. It also enforces data ownership, tagging (PII, sovereign, restricted), and access request workflows across the full lakehouse.
