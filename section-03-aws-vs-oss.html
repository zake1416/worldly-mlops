The architecture decision: AWS-first where it helps, portable where it matters.

Use AWS where it accelerates execution, keep core data layers portable where constraints are real, and avoid early choices that create expensive lock-in.

ConstraintAWS-first pathPortable pathDecision lens
China deployment Fastest if the estate stays in one cloud. Weak fit once mainland China becomes a first-class requirement. MinIO and portable inference keep the same operating pattern across Alibaba Cloud, Huawei Cloud, and AWS. Hard requirement
Data sovereignty (PIPL) Possible, but the compliance posture is more complex and more vendor-dependent. Self-hosted storage in-region keeps the legal position cleaner and easier to explain. Hard requirement
Schema evolution for Higg updates Managed warehouses move fast early, but schema-heavy change management becomes painful over time. Iceberg handles evolution in place and protects downstream consumers from constant rewrites. Annual requirement
LLM data privacy Managed endpoints are operationally convenient, but the boundary is harder to defend for sensitive supplier data. Self-hosted inference keeps supplier data inside Worldly-controlled infrastructure. Data agreements
Cost at scale Higher unit cost, lower operating overhead. Lower long-run cost, higher platform ownership burden. Tradeoff
Setup speed Faster path to a first operating baseline. Slower start, but better long-run flexibility. Tradeoff
Recommendation
AWS-first for speed. Portable core layers for control. That is the best-fit architecture: fast enough for zero-to-one execution, disciplined enough for lakehouse modernization, and flexible enough for region-aware deployment without repainting the entire platform later.
The real tradeoff is not cost alone. It is speed now versus flexibility later.

Best avoided early unless a hard constraint forces it. It adds the most operating burden at the point where the team should be learning fast and reducing uncertainty.
The goal is not to forecast exact spend. The goal is to choose an operating model that matches product constraints, team capacity, and the pace of iteration the business needs.
