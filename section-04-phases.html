The rollout path

The sequence matters more than the tooling. Data reliability comes first. Pipeline operationalization comes next. Productized ML comes after the path is trustworthy, observable, and repeatable.

Execution model: I would run this as a consultative, milestone-based program: discovery interviews, architecture mapping, proof-of-value pilots, decision memos for key tradeoffs, and slice-by-slice rollout instead of a giant-bang migration.
Phase 01
Stabilize the critical path
First
Audit first. Understand the current stack, data gaps, and failure modes before building.
Stakeholder discovery across platform, data, and product Current-state architecture mapping Pain-point inventory and failure-mode review Data quality contracts on the first critical workflow Initial raw-to-validated pipeline slice Validation gates and lineage baseline Terraform-first infrastructure baseline CI/CD operating baseline
Done when: The first critical workflow is mapped, governed, and reliably passes quality gates end to end.
Phase 02
Make delivery repeatable
Next
Build the operating layer before scaling models. Reproducible, tracked, and monitored from day one.
Repeatable orchestration and model tracking Promotion path from training to monitored serving Feature and embedding patterns teams can reuse Lakehouse operating model expanded across core regions Analytics surfaces moved onto governed data Containerized runtime and region-aware deployment Drift and operational observability baseline
Done when: A model can be trained, promoted, deployed, and monitored end to end without bespoke glue.
Phase 03
Expand into product leverage
Then
Expand into retrieval, region-aware deployment, and product scoring once the data layer is stable.
Decision-grade retrieval with citations Portable vector and inference stack Region-aware China operating model In-region data boundaries and compliance controls Product scoring and higher-value decision support
Done when: Retrieval is reliable, answers are grounded, and constrained-region deployment preserves the same operating model.
Phase 04
Scale what proves useful
After that
Scale compliance reporting, risk intelligence, and operating efficiency.
Audit-ready reporting workflows Digital product and compliance outputs Operational risk and scoring capabilities Cost-aware scaling and runtime discipline Full observability and service ownership
Done when: Reporting is audit-ready, operational burden is lower, and new product capabilities ride on trusted infrastructure.
What success looks like
A platform that is easier to trust, easier to operate, and easier to extend.
That is the point of the rollout: decision-grade data, repeatable delivery, observability-first operations, and ML capabilities that arrive on solid ground.
