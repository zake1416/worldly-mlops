How I would reduce delivery risk

A strong value proposition is not just what I would build. It is how I would keep the work understandable, reversible, and team-friendly while it is being built.

Risk
Why it's likely
Mitigation already in the plan
Data quality is worse than expected
High likelihood
At this scale, incomplete records, schema variation, and source inconsistency are normal. Quality assumptions should be tested before downstream systems depend on them.
Already mitigated
Phase 1 is audit-first. Data contracts are defined before quality gates are enforced, so bad data fails early.
GoBlu integration takes longer than planned
High likelihood
Acquisition data often arrives with undocumented schema quirks, legacy identifiers, and records that do not align cleanly with the host platform.
Already mitigated
GoBlu is isolated as a Phase 1 source track. Delay there should not block the broader MLOps foundation.
The platform becomes too dependent on one person
High likelihood
A greenfield platform owned by one person can become infrastructure only that person understands and can operate.
Explicit counter-design
Everything is defined in Terraform, Dagster, dbt, CI/CD, and runbooks. The target state is a platform the team can operate, not a system one person guards.
China regulatory requirements shift mid-build
Medium likelihood
PIPL and related localization requirements continue to evolve. That can change what is operationally or legally acceptable.
Already mitigated by architecture
The China data plane stays self-hosted and portable across Alibaba Cloud and Huawei Cloud. That reduces dependency on one vendor posture.
Model drift damages a compliance output
Medium likelihood
ESG methodologies, regulations, and supplier behavior all change over time. Silent model degradation is a real operational risk.
Already mitigated
Drift monitoring is a Phase 2 requirement. Promotion stays controlled: shadow deploy, then canary, then production.
How I would show up in the role
Transparency over silence
If an assumption is wrong or a timeline slips, I will surface it early and clearly.
Infrastructure the team can own
Everything will be documented, IaC-managed, and operable by the broader team.
Decisions, not opinions
Major tooling choices should be documented, reviewable, and tied to the constraints that matter.
